## ğŸ¨ GENERAL DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### Color Scheme Recommendations
- **Primary**: Dark Blue (#1E3A8A) - Professional, trustworthy
- **Accent**: Green (#10B981) - Success, growth, positive
- **Warning**: Orange (#F59E0B) - Attention, improvement needed
- **Danger**: Red (#EF4444) - Problems, old framework issues
- **Neutral**: Gray (#6B7280) - Supporting text

### Typography
- **Title**: Bold, 44pt
- **Subtitle**: Regular, 28pt
- **Body**: Regular, 20pt
- **Captions**: Regular, 16pt

### Layout Rules
- **White space**: Use it! Don't cram slides
- **Rule of thirds**: Important content in upper-left and center
- **Consistency**: Same layout pattern for similar slides
- **Visual hierarchy**: Biggest = most important

## ğŸ¬ ANIMATION RECOMMENDATIONS

### Slide-by-Slide Animation Strategy

**Slide 1 (Title)**
- No animation needed - Let it display immediately

**Slide 2 (Problem/Solution)**
1. Show problems first (left side)
2. Pause 2 seconds
3. Reveal solutions (right side) with "Wipe Right" effect
4. Big arrow appears between them

**Slide 3 (Business Impact)**
1. Reveal the 4 metric cards one-by-one (clockwise)
2. Pause
3. Progress bars animate from 0% to final %

**Slide 4 (Comparison)**
1. Reveal table rows one at a time
2. Each row: Old column first, then New column
3. Green checkmarks animate in with "Zoom" effect

**Slide 5 (Roadmap)**
1. "Complete" section fades in with green glow
2. "Phase 1" appears next
3. "Phase 2" appears last
4. Timeline bar fills from left to right

**Slide 6 (Call to Action)**
1. Everything displays at once (no animation)
2. Central box can have subtle "pulse" effect
3. Keep focus on the ask

### Animation Principles
- **Keep it simple**: Don't overdo animations
- **Timing**: 0.5-1 second per animation
- **Purpose**: Only animate to reveal information progressively
- **Distraction**: Avoid spinning, bouncing, or flashy effects

## ğŸ’¡ PRO TIPS FOR POWERPOINT

### Before You Start
1. **Use a template**: Choose a professional corporate template
2. **Check resolution**: 16:9 aspect ratio (modern screens)
3. **Font consistency**: Max 2 fonts (one for headings, one for body)

### While Building
1. **Align everything**: Use PowerPoint's alignment guides
2. **Consistent spacing**: Same padding/margins on all slides
3. **Test readability**: View from 10 feet away - can you read it?
4. **Color contrast**: Dark text on light background (or vice versa)

### Design Shortcuts
- **Ctrl+D**: Duplicate selected object
- **Shift+Drag**: Maintain aspect ratio when resizing
- **Alt+Drag**: Duplicate and move object
- **Ctrl+Shift+C/V**: Copy/paste formatting

### What Makes Slides Look Professional
âœ… Consistent alignment (everything lines up)
âœ… Generous white space (not cluttered)
âœ… High-contrast colors (easy to read)
âœ… Simple icons (not clipart)
